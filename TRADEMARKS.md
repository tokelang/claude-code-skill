## Why this matters

Open-source code and brand identity are different legal layers. The Apache 2.0 license gives you full code rights. The trademark protects users from confusion about which "Tokelang" they're getting. If you fork the code, your fork needs its own name — that way nobody installs your version expecting our version, and vice versa.

This is the standard model for open-source dev tools (Linux, MariaDB, PostgreSQL, Mozilla all do the same).
